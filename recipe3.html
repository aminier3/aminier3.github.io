Chocolate

Chip Cookies



History

Chocolate chip cookies were created in the late 1930's by chef Ruth Graves,in Whitman Massachusetts. Chocolate chip cookies were created on accident when Ruth used used a subsitute chopped up Nestle chocolate bar. It was originally called Toll House Crunch Cookies and popular during World War ll.



Ingredients


  • All purpose flour- 2 1/4 cups
  • Baking soda- 1 teaspoon
  • Cornstarch- 1 1/2 teaspoons
  • Salt- 1/2 teaspoon
  • Unsalted butter- 3/4 cup
    • melted and cooled-5 minutes
  • Brown Sugar- 3/4 cup
    • light or dark
  • Granulated sugar- 1/2 cup
  • 1 large egg
  • Vanilla extract- 2 teaspoons
  • Chocolate chips- 1 1/4 cups
    • semi-sweet or or chocolate chunks

Directions


  1. Whisk together the flour, baking soda, cornstarch, and salt in a large bowl. Set it aside.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the melted butter, brown sugar, and granulated sugar until there are no lumps. Whisk in the egg
  3. Then, pour in the vanilla extract.
  4. Then mix it in with the dry ingredients and stir.
  5. After that, fold in the chocolate chips.
  6. Cover the dough lightly and place in fridge for 2-3 hours or up to 3 days.
  7. Take out the dough and let it get to room temperature (about 10 minutes).
  8. Preheat over to 325 degrees.
  9. Line large baking sheets with parchment paper and put it aside.
  10. Roll dough into balls and place on baking sheet.
  11. Bake cookies for 10-12 minutes or until the edges are slightly brown.
  12. Take the cookies out and let cool for about 10 minutes.
  13. Serve and enjoy!






Tips and Tricks

What makes the perfect Chocolate Chip Cookie?

  • Use good chocolate. It makes all the difference!
  • Age the dough. It helps the cookies develop better flavor.
  • Folding and sifting ingredients.
  • Choose the right products and ingredients.
  • Always measure accuratly.
